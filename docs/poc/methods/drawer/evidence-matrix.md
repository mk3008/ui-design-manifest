# Primary navigation drawer evidence matrix

## Purpose and evidence rule

This matrix is for a **primary navigation drawer only**. It preserves the difference between a reusable extraction rule and a fact about the Grafana seed. A row may guide a first pass only when its evidence class and generalization boundary are explicit. Human judgment identifies priority and correction value; it is never the sole normative proof.

| ID | Candidate recommendation | Authoritative guidance | Successful-product observation | Human priority or preference | Extractor inference | Unknown or unverified claim | Generalization boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R1 | Identify the subject as a navigation drawer/panel only after observing its relationship to the workspace, rather than naming every left-side list a drawer. | [Carbon Global header](https://carbondesignsystem.com/patterns/global-header/) treats the left panel as product navigation and distinguishes the role from the header. | The frozen Grafana image shows a leading navigation region separated from the workspace. | The correction record requires established terminology and rejects a menu-bar label. | Classify the container from its visible role and representation before writing Contract prose. | Whether it is persistent, modal, or responsive cannot be proven from one static image. | **Stable method; contextual output.** The identification procedure generalizes. The container type remains observed or unknown until evidence supports it. |
| R2 | Preserve navigation hierarchy: a non-destination group is not a parent navigation item; a parent disclosure owns a child list. | [WAI-ARIA APG disclosure navigation](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/disclosure/examples/disclosure-navigation/) represents a section control and nested links as a hierarchy; it also cautions against using a menu role for ordinary site navigation. | Grafana shows grouped destinations and rows with disclosure cues in the seed evidence. | The human correction separated group labels from real parent items because conflation changed the intended structure. | Normalize observed rows into group, parent item, disclosure trigger, child list, child item, or plain item. | The source's programmatic ownership and keyboard behavior are not visible in the screenshot. | **Stable method; contextual pattern.** The distinction generalizes; the presence of disclosure, labels, and nesting remains source-specific until observed. |
| R3 | Record a current destination as an explicit state with a visual distinction and an accessibility requirement; do not require one particular decoration. | [WAI ARIA26](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/aria/ARIA26) documents `aria-current` for the visually highlighted current page in navigation. | A selected row may combine a leading accent with fill, text, or icon cues. | The human valued a state that is not color-only and corrected an ambiguous treatment. | Capture accent presence, placement (including leading/left edge when observed), shape/role, and coexistence with other cues as separate observations. | Whether the source exposes `aria-current` is not proven by a static image. | **Stable outcome; source-specific treatment.** A distinct current state generalizes; its exact color, width, geometry, and cue combination do not. |
| R4 | Treat a disclosure as a control plus content with an explicit expanded/collapsed state; keep it independent from drawer visibility. | [APG Disclosure pattern](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/disclosure/) describes the controlled content and `aria-expanded`; [APG Treeview](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/treeview/) assigns `aria-expanded` only to parent nodes that own child groups. | A drawer may show indicator placement and direction, but a static observation does not establish interaction. | The human correction required parent/disclosure state not be mistaken for group membership or drawer visibility. | Distinguish caption, leaf, and expandable parent; record indicator presence, placement, direction, synchronization, icon responsibility, and accessible state. | A static image does not establish activation, focus, or state persistence. | **Stable method; implementation behavior unverified.** Parent ownership and state separation generalize; actual interaction requires later validation. |
| R5 | When a compact or icon-only control is observed, capture its visible affordance separately from its required discernible name. | [APG accessible names](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/practices/names-and-descriptions/) requires focusable interactive elements to have accessible names and recommends visible text when it is appropriate. | The Grafana drawer includes compact icon-led affordances; accepted calibration tested familiar-icon treatment without copying source labels. | The human prioritized a label for ambiguous actions and an accessible name for icon-only controls. | Mark the visible treatment as observed and the discernible-name requirement as authoritative. | Familiarity of a particular icon is contextual and cannot be inferred safely from a bitmap alone. | **Stable accessibility requirement; contextual visual choice.** Do not turn “icon-only” into a default merely because it appears in the seed. |
| R6 | Check that meaningful drawer icons remain visually distinguishable in each observed state, but do not claim contrast conformance from a screenshot. | No collected authority establishes the source's contrast result; APG names guidance supports purpose, not a visual contrast measurement. | The seed and its accepted correction history show icon-led drawer controls whose foreground treatment mattered. | The human correction recorded icon foreground legibility as material. | Add a visible-legibility check and retain an explicit validation gap. | Contrast ratio, forced-colors behavior, and assistive-technology outcome are unverified. | **Contextual check; unconfirmed conformance.** It can prevent an obvious omission but is not proof of accessibility conformance. |
| R7 | Record only material color roles and their relationships; avoid exhaustive palette capture. | No authoritative drawer source collected here prescribes the seed palette. | The seed's dark canvas, drawer surface, selected surface, and leading accent were material to orientation; its exact palette belongs to that product. | The human explicitly prioritized key color roles and rejected exhaustive color capture. | Distinguish `observed role relationship` from `source-specific numeric value`. | Cross-source portability of the seed palette has not been tested. | **Source-specific calibration; stable restraint.** Do not promote the Grafana values to baseline; the extraction rule is to record only material roles. |
| R8 | Prefer a bounded, relational description over copied labels, data, measurements, or implementation mechanisms. | [Grafana Navigation](https://grafana.com/developers/saga/patterns/navigation/) describes its own information-architecture levels and states, not a portable visual clone. | The frozen Grafana image supplies useful role and hierarchy evidence, but its copy and geometry are product data. | The human correction history rejects source copying, exhaustive geometry, and CSS/framework detail. | Treat source content and geometry as excluded unless a later task defines an evidence-backed need. | Whether another product needs a different level of detail awaits cross-site validation. | **Stable method safeguard.** This is a distillation boundary, not a claim about every drawer's visual form. |
| R9 | Classify drawer presentation and visibility through four independent axes: `presentation`, `closedRepresentation`, `toggleLocation`, and `persistence`. | [Carbon Global header / left panel](https://carbondesignsystem.com/patterns/global-header/) distinguishes contextual left-panel navigation from header-only navigation; authoritative guidance does not make one visibility model universal. | The Grafana and GitLab observations show that visible, hidden, compact, and overlay representations vary by product and state. | Human review identified visibility presentation as a first-class extraction concern and rejected a single example-derived enum. | Record each axis as observed, inferred, or unknown; never derive one axis from another. | Static evidence cannot prove runtime persistence or a closed representation that is not shown. | **Stable method; contextual output.** Use only `overlay|persistent|unknown`, `dismissed|compact-rail|unknown`, `inside-drawer|outside-drawer|none-observed|unknown`, and `persistent|non-persistent|unknown` as their respective axis values. |
| R10 | Keep `dismissed` and `compact-rail` as separate closed representations, with their own control evidence and ownership seam. | No authority here selects a universal Drawer state or trigger placement. | A dismissed Drawer is fully absent; a compact rail retains a reduced region and may retain controls. | The requested method change requires a later composite check instead of guessing Header behavior. | For dismissed, record absence and the external restore owner or `unknown`; for compact-rail, record retained region, internal control, and only declared shortcuts. | Static Drawer evidence cannot prove Header trigger placement, coordinated state, or persistence. | **Stable observation method; contextual output.** A later Header+Drawer composite validation proves the seam; this method does not implement Header extraction. |
| R11 | Permit a disclosure-indicator fallback only for a contract-declared expandable parent lacking an asset. | [APG Disclosure pattern](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/disclosure/) describes rightward indication for hidden content and downward indication for visible content. | No product icon family is portable evidence. | The requested method change rejects text glyph and placeholder substitutions. | Use a meaningful `currentColor` inline SVG chevron/caret, right when collapsed and down when expanded, synchronized with the accessible state. | Whether a fallback is needed, and its product asset, remain Contract inputs. | **Stable fallback constraint; contextual rendering.** Never use it for captions or leaves, and never treat it as a copied or universal icon. |

## Freeze boundary

Before the next successful product is selected, human review must accept or revise this foundation. Then freeze the template and prompt version and apply that unchanged version to a different product's **drawer only**. Use the later result to classify each candidate rule as stable, contextual, source-specific, omitted, or unsupported; do not silently revise the extractor while evaluating that holdout.
